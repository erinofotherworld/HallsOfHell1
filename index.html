*Lupercallia Celebration, 15th of Febuary 12026HE: - Lupercallia Celebration: Lupercallia is an ancient Roman festival celebrated on February 15th, dedicated to Lupercus, the god of fertility and shepherds. The festival is marked by rituals intended to purify the city and promote fertility among its inhabitants. Participants, often dressed in goat skins, would run through the streets, striking people with strips of goat hide, believed to bring fertility and ward off evil spirits.

For Luciferians and Satanists, Lupercallia can be seen as a celebration of life's primal forces, the wild and untamed aspects of nature, and the embrace of one's own desires and instincts as part of the natural order. It is a time to honor the cycles of life, death, and rebirth, and to connect with the raw energy that drives existence - but with less striking people with goat skins... thats a tad weird to this wee lass, mmm'kay?

*Walpurgisnacht Celebration, 30th of April 12026HE: An older Northern European festival celebrated on the night of April 30th, Walpurgisnacht is associated with the arrival of spring and the warding off of evil spirits. Traditionally, it is believed that witches gather on this night to celebrate and perform rituals. The festival often includes bonfires, dancing, and various festivities to welcome the changing season. Its not unlike Beltane in many regards, the tradition stems with Walpurgis, an 8th-century abbess who was later canonized as a saint who was known for her healing abilities and protection against witchcraft.

Luciferians and Satanists may view Walpurgisnacht as a celebration of personal freedom, the rejection of societal norms, and the embrace of one's own path. It is a time to honor the transformative power of spring - the time when life return to the world - and to celebrate the strength and resilience of the individual spirit.
