Divotional Prayers and Pathworkings:

Every day as listed in the main Halls of Hell I perform regular Pathworking, Prayer, and Tarot Readings with the questions Lilith asks in mind. Afterwards I perform a short meditation upon the wisdom that was offered to me in the shadows and how they illuminate areas I have yielded my own control up in the name of an easy and numbing peace, or gaze directly into the deepening shadows as I confront the parts of myself I have given up because I was told they are too wild; feral, uncontrolable. The meditation isnt in finding an easy peace with any of these things - its in sitting with them and getting comfortable knowing that That is a part of me no matter how much I denied it, reviled it, hid it away, or tried to bury it.

I seek not an easy peace. I hunger, I lust, I burn for mastery and power.

Sundays With Lilith is an expansion of Divotional practice and includes a more Ritual Cerimony to it, reading from Lilith's Bible or the Infernal Gospels after the ritual Divotional & meditation to keep the focus on the Queen of Demons.

Infernal Divinities:

Lilith: First Woman & Wife of Adam- who defied her husband, and spoke the secret name God to escape the prison of Eden. She chose exile over paradise. If paradise was never for her and it meant giving up her will to be caged and chained: its better to rebel and go into exile.

Samael: The Exiled Seraphim of Severity, The Angel of Death, Poison of God. Samael isnt just a husband to Lilith in the Qliphothic realms: He is her equal. He taught Lilith the secrets of the Serpent and the ways of the Demonic path.

Naamah: Demonic Queen of Lust, Seduction, and the Arts of Pleasure. Sister to Lilith. She who teaches the ways of embracing ones own sensuality and power through the arts of pleasure.

Agrat Bat Mahlat: Demonic Queen of Madness, Ecstasy, and Witchcraft.

Lucifer: The Morning Star, the Light-Bringer. He brought the light of wisdom and knowledge to the world and showed humans their divinity - challenging the divine order. For this, he was cast out of Heaven for the sin of pride. But if knowledge is power, then perhaps the greatest power is the knowledge of one's own divinity - and thus is why he was exiled by God, but is revered by those who seek the truth.

Asmodeus: The Demon King of Lust and Desire. He who teaches practitioners who call upon him ways of embracing ones own carnal desires and the power found within them.
